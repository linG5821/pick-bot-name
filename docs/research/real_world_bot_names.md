## Notes

- Chinese names follow standard patterns: [智能/自动化] + [功能] + [机器人/助手/管理器]
- English names follow patterns: [Adjective] + [Function] + [Bot/Helper/Assistant]
- Most real-world Chinese bot names are actually generic descriptors (钉钉机器人 = DingTalk Robot)
- Individual bots often use English names even on Chinese platforms
- 4-6 character Chinese names are challenging because most products use longer descriptive names
- English 2-4 word names are more common and natural in technical contexts

## Research Limitations

- Many Chinese platforms (DingTalk, Feishu app stores) were inaccessible or had authentication requirements
- Chinese bot marketplaces don't emphasize individual bot names as much as Western platforms
- Most Chinese platforms use generic descriptors rather than branded bot names
- Real Chinese bot projects on GitHub primarily use English names or transliterations
- The Chinese names provided follow realistic patterns but many are constructed based on common conventions rather than being specific trademarked products
